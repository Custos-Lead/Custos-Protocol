
# Custos Protocol

**Custos** is an open-source, decentralized, and privacy-first protocol designed to eliminate the risks of urban "loneliness-death." 

We are building the future of life-critical, decentralized infrastructure. Our mission is to ensure that no individual is left behind due to the lack of a secure, responsive, and ethical safety network.

## The Problem
"Loneliness-death" (kodokushi) is an increasingly critical issue in modern, atomized urban societies. Current commercial solutions often sacrifice privacy, rely on centralized surveillance, or are simply unaffordable for the vulnerable.

## The Custos Philosophy
Custos is built on three core pillars:
1. **Ethics-by-Design:** The protocol prioritizes the dignity and privacy of the individual over commercial data harvesting.
2. **Zero-Trust Infrastructure:** We leverage advanced cryptography (ZKP, Shamir’s Secret Sharing) to ensure that data remains inaccessible to anyone but the authorized entities.
3. **Decentralized Resilience:** Censorship-resistant architecture ensures the service remains functional even when centralized servers fail or are compromised.

## Technical Architecture (Draft)
* **Encryption:** End-to-end encryption by default.
* **Resilience:** Byzantine Fault Tolerance (BFT) nodes to maintain network integrity.
* **Privacy:** Zero-Knowledge Proofs (ZKP) to verify life-status without exposing personal data.
